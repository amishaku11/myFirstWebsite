Dinosaur Extinction

Dinosaurs dominated Earth for over 165 million years, but around 66 million years ago, they disappeared suddenly in a dramatic event called the Cretaceous-Paleogene (K-Pg) extinction.

Causes of Extinction

1. Asteroid Impact: A massive asteroid, over 10 km wide, struck the YucatÃ¡n Peninsula in Mexico. This caused huge fires, tsunamis, and dust that blocked sunlight.
2. Volcanic Activity: Massive eruptions in the Deccan Traps of India released lava and gases, changing the climate drastically.
3. Climate Change: Sudden shifts in temperature and vegetation made survival difficult for large animals.

Effects on Dinosaurs

1. Most dinosaur species, including the mighty T-Rex and Triceratops, went extinct.
2. Only a few species survived, which eventually evolved into modern birds.
3. Ecosystems changed forever, allowing mammals and eventually humans to thrive.
