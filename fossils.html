Dinosaur Fossils

Fossils are the preserved remains or traces of ancient life. They are like time capsules, helping scientists understand what dinosaurs looked like, how they lived, and how the Earth has changed over millions of years.

What Are Dinosaur Fossils?

1. Dinosaur fossils include bones, teeth, eggs, and sometimes even skin impressions.
2. Fossils are formed when a dinosaur dies and is buried under layers of mud and sand. Over time, minerals replace the organic material, turning it into stone.
3. Fossils provide crucial evidence about extinct species, their size, diet, and even behavior.

Famous Fossil Discoveries

1. Archaeopteryx: A fossil showing the link between dinosaurs and birds.
2. Sue the T-Rex: One of the most complete Tyrannosaurus Rex skeletons ever found.
3. Velociraptor with feathers: Shows that some dinosaurs had feathers, not scales.

How Fossils Are Studied

1. Paleontologists carefully excavate fossils from rock layers.
2. They use tools like brushes, chisels, and scanners to examine the bones.
