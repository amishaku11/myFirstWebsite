Step into the prehistoric age and meet the legendary giants that once ruled the Earth! From fierce hunters to gentle plant-eaters, dinosaurs came in all shapes and sizes. Here are some of the most famous dinosaurs that continue to amaze scientists and dino-lovers around the world.

I. Tyrannosaurus Rex (T-Rex)

Known as the King of the Dinosaurs, the Tyrannosaurus Rex was one of the most powerful predators to ever walk the planet.
It lived about 68 to 66 million years ago during the Late Cretaceous period.
With a massive skull, sharp teeth, and strong legs, it could crush bones and run surprisingly fast for its size.
The T-Rex became world-famous through movies like Jurassic Park.

II. Triceratops

The Triceratops was a plant-eating dinosaur known for its large frill and three sharp facial horns.
It lived around 68 million years ago in North America.
Its horns were used to defend against predators like T-Rex.
Despite its tough look, it was a peaceful herbivore that moved in herds.

III. Velociraptor

The Velociraptor was a small, fast, and intelligent predator.
It was about the size of a turkey but had deadly claws and hunted in packs.
It lived around 75 million years ago during the Late Cretaceous period.
Real Velociraptors had feathers, unlike their movie versions!

IV. Brachiosaurus

The Brachiosaurus was one of the tallest dinosaurs ever discovered.
It had long front legs and a long neck that allowed it to eat leaves from tall trees.
This gentle giant lived about 154 million years ago.

VI. Spinosaurus

The Spinosaurus was even longer than the T-Rex and had a large sail-like spine on its back.

Scientists consider it the largest known carnivorous dinosaur.
